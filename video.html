Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung Palace, built in 1395, stands as a majestic symbol of Korea’s Joseon Dynasty. King Sejong, one of Korea’s most revered rulers, known for creating the Hangul script, is featured here in an immersive historical setting, emphasizing his contributions to science, culture, and governance.

Edinburgh Castle

Edinburgh Castle, perched on Castle Rock, serves as a backdrop to the turbulent medieval power struggles. This scene features Robert Stewart, Duke of Albany, and England’s King Richard II, encapsulating a critical chapter in Scotland’s storied history of alliances and rivalries

The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, was commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan as a memorial to his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Ustad Ahmad Lahauri, its chief architect, designed this marble masterpiece, blending Mughal, Persian, and Indian architectural styles to create one of the world's greatest monuments of love.
